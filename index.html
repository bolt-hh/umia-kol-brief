What Umia is

A platform for funding and governing on-chain ventures, with each token anchored to the venture behind it.

Most launches peak at listing and drift from there. Umia treats the sale as where a venture begins, not where it ends. A project raises into a non-custodial treasury founders cannot withdraw from at will, with the token tied to that treasury, the venture's revenue, and its IP through a legal structure.

Three parts make this work. A legal wrapper, built with MetaLeX, anchors the token to the venture. A Tailored Auction, built on Uniswap's Continuous Clearing Auction, handles open price discovery while a project verifies bidders privately through the Umia browser extension. Decision markets let participants price the expected impact of a venture's decisions.

Umia is incubated by Chainbound, an Ethereum research and development lab, and is the first venture to launch on its own platform. Contracts are audited by Certora.

Why Umia matters

Decision markets, a new primitive

Non-custodial by design

The $UMIA token

$UMIA is the native token of the Umia platform. It is the asset sold in the auction and lets holders take part in decision markets over the platform's treasury and direction. Sale and liquidity are built to be liquid from the first block, with team and backer allocations under long-term, performance-based unlocks.
